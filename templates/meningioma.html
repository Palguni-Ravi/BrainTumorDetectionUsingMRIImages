Treatment Options

  • Observation: In some cases, small and asymptomatic meningiomas may be monitored closely without immediate treatment.
  • Surgery: Surgical removal is often the primary treatment for meningiomas. The goal is to completely remove the tumor while preserving neurological function.
  • Radiation therapy: Radiation may be used after surgery to destroy any remaining tumor cells or as the primary treatment when surgery is not possible.
  • Medications: Certain hormonal therapies may be used for meningiomas that are hormone-sensitive.
